>_ 10 MOST COMMON PASSWORDS (AND WHY THEY'RE DANGEROUS)

Every year, cybersecurity companies release the list of most common passwords. The results are always terrifying — and predictable.

📋 The Top 10 (2025)

  1. 123456
  2. password
  3. 123456789
  4. 12345
  5. 12345678
  6. qwerty
  7. abc123
  8. password1
  9. 111111
  10. 123123

💀 Why They're Dangerous

A hacker's computer can try billions of passwords per second. Here's how fast these common passwords fall:

  • "123456" → Less than 1 second to crack
  • "password" → Less than 1 second
  • "qwerty" → Less than 1 second
  • "abc123" → Less than 1 second

If your password is on this list, it's not a password — it's an invitation.

🔓 How Hackers Exploit This

Hackers use what's called a "dictionary attack". They take lists of millions of leaked passwords and try them against accounts. If your password is common, it's in that list. They don't need to "guess" — they already have it.

🛡️ How to Protect Yourself

  • ✅ Use 12+ characters — longer is exponentially harder to crack
  • ✅ Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols
  • ✅ Use a password manager to generate and store unique passwords
  • ✅ Never reuse passwords across sites
  • ✅ Enable 2FA (two-factor authentication) everywhere possible
